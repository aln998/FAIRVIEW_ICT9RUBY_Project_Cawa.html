What is a Fire?

Fire is the result of a combustion, which is a chemical reaction. It happens when oxygen and some type of fuel mix together. In order for combustion to happen, the fuel must be heated to its ignition temperature. With enough heat, oxygen, and fuel, the fire will keep going.

Our Purpose

Fires are not fully unexpected, there are many things that we can observe in order to prevent a possible fire. With these few tips, we can help you prevent fires from happening in your home.


House Fire Prevention Tips

    Unplug Idle Chargers
 Chargers, Portable Appliances, any of that sort is recommended to be unplugged when not in use. This is because leaving them overnight might cause overheating, and could result in the ignition of a fire. It is also recommended as they use a lot of power from the outlets, so unplugging them can simultaneously reduce the risk of fire, but also reduce electricity bills.
    Clean Your Appliances
 Make sure to regularly clean your appliances, especially those related with cooking. Cleaning them is important in order to prevent grease from building up. Grease fires may happen, which come from hot cooking oil. Dust may also spread if appliances are not clean, which is another cause of fire ignition.
    Be Careful with Flaming Objects
 When using open flame decorations, such as candles and oil lamps, it is recommended and best to cover them with a wire cage. Make sure to place them away from flammable objects or things that may catch fire such as paper, curtains, and appliances.
